The first question every vault asks is the right one: *do we have to give up
custody?* The answer is no. The standard is designed so that the metal stays in
approved custody and the vault remains the authoritative registrar.

## The vault stays the registrar

Each participating vault operates its own Title Register — the authoritative,
on-chain record of allocated bars in its custody. The protocol does not take
possession of bars and does not become the registrar.

1. The vault registers eligible bars.
2. The vault approves title events under Guardian-controlled policy.
3. Approved events are published on-chain for transparency.

## Why this matters

Legibility, not possession, is what unlocks liquidity. Once title is legible to
the wider market under one shared standard, allocated gold can reach a global
network without ever leaving the vault.
